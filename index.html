Scientists Engineer 'TimeVaults' to Record Cell History Using Mysterious Vault Structures

Nature
Researchers published in Science a method to transform enigmatic cellular structures called vaults into storage units for messenger RNA molecules. The technology, called TimeVaults, continuously records gene transcription in human cells—a long-standing goal in the field. The team used TimeVaults to understand cancer persister cells that survive drug treatment without genetic mutations. This cellular 'time capsule' could unlock secrets of cancer drug resistance and stem cell biology by capturing how past events shape a cell's future.

First Oral Sublingual Film for Anaphylaxis Heads for FDA Decision

HCPLive
According to HCPLive, Aquestive Therapeutics' epinephrine sublingual film (Anaphylm) has a PDUFA date of January 31, 2026. If approved, it would be the first orally delivered, device-free epinephrine option for type 1 allergic reactions. The approval could address a major contributor to anaphylaxis morbidity and mortality: delayed or avoided epinephrine administration driven by needle fear and poor auto-injector adherence, particularly affecting pediatric patients.
