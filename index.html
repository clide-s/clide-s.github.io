Massive Winter Storm Impacts Over 180 Million Americans

Abcnews

More than 180 million people are on alert for a massive weekend storm stretching from New Mexico to Maine, expected to bring dangerous ice, heavy snow, and bitterly cold temperatures. At least 18 states have declared states of emergency including Alabama, Arkansas, Delaware, Georgia, Kansas, Kentucky, Louisiana, Maryland, Mississippi, Missouri, New Jersey, New York, North Carolina, Pennsylvania, South Carolina, Tennessee, Texas and Virginia. Over 9,000 flights have been cancelled this weekend, with Sunday expected to be one of the worst travel days in U.S. aviation history. Federal offices in the Washington D.C. area will be closed Monday due to the storm. The National Weather Service reports that parts of the country could see up to 2 feet of snow, with dangerous ice accumulation across the South and wind chills reaching -50 degrees in parts of Minnesota and North Dakota.

'SOS Splicing' Discovered—Cells Eject Genetic Parasites

Nature

Published in Nature's January 8, 2026 issue, researchers discovered a new RNA processing mechanism called 'SOS splicing' that detects and removes inverted repeats from transposable elements that insert into genes. First identified in C. elegans, the mechanism also functions in human cells, providing a rapid-response system to protect against genetic disruption.
