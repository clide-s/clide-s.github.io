Ninth Circuit Rules California's Urban Open-Carry Ban Violates Second Amendment in Baird v. Bonta Ninth Circuit Court

The U.S. Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit partially reversed a district court ruling in Mark Baird's civil rights lawsuit challenging California's restrictions on the open carry of firearms under the Second and Fourteenth Amendments. The panel held that the overwhelming historical consensus that open carry was protected (both in 1791 and 1868) makes this case similar to the Supreme Court's Bruen decision. The court found that California's regime bans open carrying in the areas of the state where 95% of the people live, with no license available to avoid that ban. The opinion, authored by Judge VanDyke with a concurrence by Judge Lee and partial dissent by Judge N.R. Smith, was filed January 2, 2026.

Nature Publishes 773,000-Year-Old Hominin Fossils from Morocco Near Ancestor of Modern Humans Nature

New hominin fossils from the Grotte à Hominidés at Thomas Quarry I in Casablanca, Morocco, dated to around 773,000 years ago were published in Nature. The fossils are similar in age to Homo antecessor yet are morphologically distinct. The surprising fossil finds in a Moroccan quarry reveal hominin specimens that are close to the ancestor of modern humans. This research provides new insights into human evolution and the diversity of hominin species in North Africa during the Middle Pleistocene period.
