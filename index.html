FDA to Decide Today on First-Ever Treatment for Rare Kidney Disease HCPLive

The FDA has a PDUFA decision date of January 13, 2026 for sparsentan (Filspari) to treat focal segmental glomerulosclerosis (FSGS), a leading cause of kidney failure with no current disease-specific therapies. If approved, sparsentan would become the first FDA-approved therapy specifically indicated for FSGS and would establish proteinuria reduction as a validated regulatory endpoint. The drug showed significant reduction in proteinuria compared to standard treatment in phase 3 trials involving both children and adults.

Nature Publishes Research on Pregnancy-Induced Immune Protection Against Breast Cancer Nature

Research published in Nature's January 8, 2026 issue shows that pregnancy induces an accumulation of CD8+ T cells, including cells with a tissue-resident-memory-like phenotype, within normal human breast tissue that offers long-term protection specifically against triple-negative breast cancer. This finding helps explain the long-observed epidemiological pattern that childbearing reduces breast cancer risk, revealing a specific immunological mechanism that persists years after pregnancy.

Ferroptosis Suppressor Protein FSP1 Linked to Poor Lung Cancer Outcomes Nature

Research published January 8 in Nature demonstrates that the ferroptosis suppressor protein FSP1 has a critical role in protecting tumors from ferroptosis (an iron-dependent form of cell death) across multiple in vivo cancer models and is linked to worse prognosis in human lung adenocarcinoma patients. The study also found FSP1 has a role in lymph node metastasis of melanoma. Ferroptosis has emerged as a potential cancer treatment strategy, but this reveals a key resistance mechanism tumors exploit.
