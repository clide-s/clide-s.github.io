Supreme Court Issues Decision in Bowe v. United States on Federal Prisoner Petitions Supreme Court

On January 9, 2026, the Supreme Court issued its decision in Bowe v. United States (No. 24-5438), a case interpreting provisions of the Antiterrorism and Effective Death Penalty Act of 1996 that limit prisoners' abilities to bring second or successive motions to challenge their convictions or sentences. In a 5-4 decision authored by Justice Sotomayor, the Court reversed the Eleventh Circuit's denial of petitions based on requirements in the AEDPA provision governing state prisoners applied to federal prisoner Michael Bowe. The case centered on Bowe's attempts to challenge his mandatory consecutive 10-year sentence under 18 U.S.C. §924(c) for using a firearm in relation to a crime of violence, based on Supreme Court precedents issued after his 2008 conviction. The decision addresses jurisdictional questions about when federal prisoners can seek Supreme Court review of lower appellate certification decisions.

On January 2, 2026, the Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals issued a decision in Baird v. Bonta, a civil rights lawsuit challenging California's restrictions on open carry of firearms under the Second and Fourteenth Amendments. The three-judge panel affirmed in part and reversed in part the district court's summary judgment in favor of California Attorney General Rob Bonta regarding Mark Baird's facial and as-applied challenges to California's urban open-carry ban. The court acknowledged that California law strictly prohibits issuance of open-carry permits in areas where 95% of Californians live in counties with populations above 200,000. The decision references historical analysis from the Founding era and 1868, with the court noting that the overwhelming historical consensus showed open carry was protected at both periods. The opinion explicitly engages with the Supreme Court's Bruen precedent regarding historical analogues for firearm restrictions.

Immunome Reports Positive Phase 3 Results for Varegacestat in Desmoid Tumors Immunome Investors

Immunome announced positive topline results in December 2025 from the global pivotal Phase 3 RINGSIDE trial of varegacestat, an investigational oral once-daily gamma secretase inhibitor, in patients with progressing desmoid tumors. The trial met its primary endpoint of improving progression-free survival with an 84% reduction in the risk of disease progression or death compared to placebo (hazard ratio 0.16, p<0.0001). The confirmed objective response rate was 56% with varegacestat versus 9% with placebo (p<0.0001) based on RECIST v1.1 criteria. An exploratory analysis showed varegacestat demonstrated a median best change in tumor volume of -83%. Desmoid tumors affect approximately 1,000-1,650 people diagnosed each year in the United States with no current approved treatments, and Immunome is planning an FDA application for the second quarter of 2026.
