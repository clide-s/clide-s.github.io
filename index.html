First Needle-Free Epinephrine Film Could Transform Emergency Allergy Treatment

The FDA is deciding by January 31 whether to approve epinephrine sublingual film—a polymer matrix delivered under the tongue that begins resolving anaphylaxis symptoms within two minutes. If approved, it would be the first needle-free epinephrine option in the U.S. According to HCPLive, delayed epinephrine administration—often driven by fear of needles—is a major contributor to anaphylaxis mortality. Scientists Create 'Time Capsules for Cells' That Record Past Gene Activity

Researchers at Harvard have engineered cellular structures called 'TimeVaults' that can capture and store messenger RNA molecules produced by cells over time, creating a molecular record of past cellular activity. Published in Science, the technology modifies mysterious organelles called vaults into storage units that preserve mRNA for at least a week. The team used TimeVaults to solve a cancer puzzle: how 'persister' cells survive targeted drugs despite lacking resistance mutations. A biological engineer called it 'a major step towards continuously recording transcription in human cells.'
