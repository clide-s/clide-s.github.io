Transposable DNA Elements Essential for Brain Development

Research published in Science identified PGBD5, a transposase-derived gene, as essential for normal brain development in mice and humans. The study found that deficiency of PGBD5 can cause intellectual disabilities, movement disorders, and epilepsy. This reveals that DNA transposable elements—often dismissed as 'junk DNA'—actively contribute to genome rearrangement during brain development, establishing a conceptual link between genome dynamics, neuronal diversity, and neurodevelopmental disease.
