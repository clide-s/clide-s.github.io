 CBS News

 The Daily Record

The en banc decision reversed a January 2025 three-judge panel ruling, siding with major airlines who argued federal law gave DOT no such authority. The ruling eliminates consumer protections that would have made hidden fees visible before purchase. Per the court filing, this affects how airlines interact with customers across the booking process. FDA Faces February Decision Deadline on First Gene Therapy for Hunter Syndrome

 The Cardiology Advisor
The FDA's February 8 PDUFA date approaches for clemidsogene lanparvovec, which would be the first gene therapy for mucopolysaccharidosis II (Hunter syndrome), a rare X-linked disorder causing enzyme deficiency and organ dysfunction. The one-time AAV therapy delivers the missing gene directly to the central nervous system. Multiple medical outlets published decision previews on February 3, reflecting heightened attention to this potential first-in-class approval. The decision comes amid broader FDA uncertainty under Commissioner Makary, with analysts noting 'slipping timelines' and unpredictability in reviews.
